Pronouns in malayalam

These are the words that you use to refer to people, places, animals or things.

First person

These are words used to refer to yourself.

Malayalam word Pronunciation English meaning Example sentence
Njan njaan I / Me Njan oru pusthakam vaayichu. (I read a book.)
Ente en-te My Ente veedu ivide aanu. (My house is here.)
Nammal nam-mal We (inclusive. Us, both you and me) Nammal kootayirunnu. (We were together.)
Njangal njan-gal We (exclusive. Us, but not you.) Nammal kootayirunnu. (We were together.)

Second person

These are words used to describe the person who you're talking to.

Malayalam Word Pronunciation English Meaning Example Sentence
Nee nee You (informal) Nee evide poyi? (Where did you go?)
Ninte nin-te Your (informal) Ninte peru entha? (What is your name?)
Ningal ning-gal You (formal/plural) Ningal entha vicharichathu? (What did you think?)

Third person

These are words used to describe someone/something that's not you or the peson you're talking to.

Malayalam word Pronunciation English meaning Example sentence
Avan ah-van He (informal / singular male) Avan school-il poyi. (He went to school.)
Avante ah-van-te His (belonging to him) Avante book table-il undu. (His book is on the table.)
Aval ah-vahl She (informal / singular female) Aval oru doctor aanu. (She is a doctor.)
Avalde ah-vahl-de Hers (belonging to her) Avalde pen nashichu. (Her pen broke.)
Avar ah-var They / Them (polite plural or respectful) Avar innu varilla. (They are not coming today.)
Avarde ah-var-de Theirs (belonging to them) Avarde veedu valiyaanu. (Their house is big.)
Ivar ee-var These people (nearby, respectful) Ivar ente koottukaar aanu. (These are my friends.)
Ivarde ee-var-de Theirs (belonging to these people) Ivarde samsaaram manoharam aanu. (Their speech is beautiful.)
Athu ah-thu It / That (neuter, far) Athu oru pazham aanu. (That is a fruit.)
Athinte ah-thin-te Its / That one's (possessive) Athinte niram chuvappu aanu. (Its color is red.)
Athokke ah-tho-ke All those / All that Athokke njan kandirunnu. (I saw all that.)
Ithu ee-thu This (neuter, near) Ithu enikku ishtamaanu. (I like this.)
Ithinte ee-thin-te This one's / Its (possessive) Ithinte vila randu rupa aanu. (Its price is two rupees.)
Ithokke ee-tho-ke All these / All this Ithokke njan konduvannirikkunnu. (I have brought all this.)
